# Liven Facebook Ads — Hooks & Messaging Analysis

**Analysis Date:** December 7, 2024  
**Source:** Meta Ad Library + Funnel Documentation  
**Active Ads:** ~830 (as of analysis date)  
**Platforms:** Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, Audience Network, Threads

---

## Executive Summary

Liven runs a massive ad operation (~830 active ads) with highly segmented messaging across multiple languages and demographics. Their ad strategy focuses on **problem-aware pain points**, **social proof at scale**, and **personalization promises**.

---

## Core Messaging Themes

### Theme 1: Personalization Promise
**Core Message:** "Your plan is built specifically for you"

**Liven Examples:**
- "A PERSONALIZED WELL-BEING MANAGEMENT PLAN"
- "IMPROVE YOUR WELL-BEING WITH OUR PERSONALIZED PLAN"
- "Enter your email to see your personal Profile Summary"
- "Your personal Well-being Management Plan is ready!"

**Why It Works:**
- Creates curiosity (what will MY plan say?)
- Implies uniqueness (not generic advice)
- Justifies quiz length (we need info to personalize)

---

### Theme 2: Social Proof at Scale
**Core Message:** "Millions of people trust us"

**Liven Examples:**
- "Over 2,500,000 people have chosen Liven"
- "Join over 2,500,000 people"
- Diverse user avatars showing community

**Why It Works:**
- Bandwagon effect (if millions use it, it must work)
- Reduces perceived risk
- Creates FOMO

---

### Theme 3: Scientific Authority
**Core Message:** "This is backed by science, not guesswork"

**Liven Examples:**
- "Liven was developed using scientific practices"
- "Your plan will be reviewed by our science team"
- "How much do you know about Behavioral Techniques?"
- Expert endorsements with credentials

**Why It Works:**
- Differentiates from "woo-woo" wellness
- Implies medical-grade effectiveness
- Builds trust for skeptical audiences

---

### Theme 4: Pain Point Naming
**Core Message:** "We understand exactly what you're going through"

**Liven Examples (from quiz questions used as hooks):**
- "How often do you feel tired or lack energy, even after rest?"
- "Do you often leave things to the last minute?"
- "How often do you feel worried or overwhelmed?"
- "I often feel overwhelmed by the amount of tasks I have to do"
- "Have you ever struggled with accepting compliments?"
- "Do you often prioritize others' needs and sacrifice your own?"

**Why It Works:**
- Creates "that's me!" recognition
- Validates their experience
- Implies the product addresses their specific issue

---

### Theme 5: Feature-Benefit Framing
**Core Message:** "Here's what you get and why it matters"

**Liven Examples:**
- "Train your nervous system to handle emotions"
- "Recognize emotional triggers to start changing"
- "Disarm your inner critic to rebuild self-esteem"
- "Get validation and emotional support 24/7"
- "Develop a deep sense of safety and calm"

**Why It Works:**
- Connects feature to outcome
- Uses action verbs (train, recognize, disarm)
- Paints picture of transformed state

---

### Theme 6: Universal Truth Opening
**Core Message:** "Life is hard, we get it"

**Liven Examples:**
- "Life comes without a manual"

**Why It Works:**
- Immediately relatable
- Non-threatening entry point
- Sets up "but here's help" pivot

## Visual Ad Patterns

### Pattern 1: Before/After
- Split screen showing transformation
- Stressed person → Calm person
- Works for emotional benefit messaging

### Pattern 2: Social Proof Collage
- Multiple user avatars
- Diverse demographics
- Community feeling

### Pattern 3: Quiz Preview
- Screenshot of quiz question
- Creates curiosity about own answers
- "What would you answer?"

### Pattern 4: Feature Carousel
- Multiple cards showing different features
- Swipeable format
- Works for solution-aware audiences

### Pattern 5: Testimonial Focus
- User photo + quote
- Star rating visible
- Personal story format

### Pattern 6: Expert Authority
- Photo of credentialed expert
- Professional setting
- "Dr./Therapist recommends" framing

## Ad Creative Recommendations for Memento

### Visual Style
- **Light cream backgrounds** (#F6F4F0) — matches Liven approach
- **Warm orange accents** (#E07A4F) — brand differentiation
- **Real photography** (professional, aspirational)
- **Diverse representation**
- **Calm, warm, approachable mood**

### Video Ads
- **Hook in first 3 seconds** (pain point question)
- **Show app interface briefly** (credibility)
- **End with clear CTA** (Take the quiz)
- **Subtitles mandatory** (sound-off viewing)

### Carousel Ads
- Card 1: Pain point hook
- Card 2: "What if" transformation
- Card 3: Feature highlight
- Card 4: Social proof
- Card 5: CTA

### Stories/Reels
- Vertical format (9:16)
- Text overlay hooks
- Quick cuts (attention economy)
- End with swipe-up CTA

## A/B Testing Priority

### High Priority Tests
1. Pain-first vs. Solution-first headlines
2. "2.5M users" vs. "85% satisfaction" social proof
3. "3-minute quiz" vs. "Free assessment" CTA
4. Dark visual vs. Light visual
5. Video vs. Static image

### Medium Priority Tests
1. Question hooks vs. Statement hooks
2. Long copy vs. Short copy
3. Single image vs. Carousel
4. Expert authority vs. User testimonial
5. Urgency messaging presence/absence
